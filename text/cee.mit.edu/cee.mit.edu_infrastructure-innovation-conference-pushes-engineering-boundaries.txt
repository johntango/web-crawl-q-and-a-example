engineering boundaries Published on: Dec 23, 2015 Dec 23 2015 Share  On Nov. 20, MIT hosted a full-day thought leadership forum, Infrastructure Innovation in a Changing Environment Conference, co-hosted by CEE and the Industrial Liaison Program (ILP). The event brought together 25 prominent business, government and academic luminaries to share their knowledge, insights and successes with almost 200 attendees from within MIT and beyond. “There exists a global race for the best approaches to innovate in infrastructure,” said Department Head and Conference Chair Markus Buehler, “and whoever wins the race will provide critical leadership towards a sustainable future. We will need to engage the brightest minds to develop the fundamental knowledge to drive innovation.” Read the story.   PreviousThird annual video competition  2015 News in BriefIn The News   December 23, 2015 | 2015 News in Brief | ceemit Infrastructure Innovation conference pushes engineering boundaries    December 23, 2015 | 2015 News in Brief | ceemit Third annual video competition    December 23, 2015 | 2015 News in Brief | ceemit Getting Published panel shares insights    December 23, 2015 | 2015 News in Brief | ceemit Buehler and Reis elected to Society of Engineering Science Board of Directors    December 23, 2015 | 2015 News in Brief | ceemit Students enjoy grilled cheese study break    December 16, 2015 | 2015 News in Brief | ceemit Inaugural MIT Energy Hackathon fuels teams’ competitive spirit and impressive results Subscribe to our newsletter  Email* CommentsThis field is for validation purposes and should be left unchanged.
